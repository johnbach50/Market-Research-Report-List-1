<p><p>Video surveillance systems are categorized into two main types: wireless video surveillance systems and wired video surveillance systems. </p><p>Wireless video surveillance systems utilize wireless technology to transmit video signals from the cameras to the recording and monitoring devices. This eliminates the need for physical cables, providing more flexibility in camera placement and reducing installation costs. It is ideal for areas where running cables is challenging or not feasible.</p><p>On the other hand, wired video surveillance systems require physical cables to connect the cameras to the recording and monitoring devices. This type of system ensures a stable and reliable connection and is commonly used in areas where constant surveillance is necessary, such as high-security facilities or large commercial complexes.</p></p>
