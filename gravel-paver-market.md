<p><p>Gravel paver, also known as permeable paver or Porous Pave, is a type of paving material designed to allow water to infiltrate into the ground rather than running off. It consists of interlocking plastic or concrete pavers, filled with small stones or gravel. These pavers have voids that allow water to pass through, preventing water accumulation and promoting natural drainage.</p><p>The gravel paver market has been experiencing significant growth over the past few years and is expected to continue its upward trajectory. The increasing awareness about sustainability and the need for eco-friendly paving solutions are driving the demand for gravel pavers. These pavers are often used in various applications, such as driveways, parking lots, pedestrian walkways, and patios, as they offer advantages like reducing stormwater runoff, minimizing the heat island effect, and preventing soil erosion.</p><p>Moreover, government initiatives promoting sustainable infrastructure development and increasing investments in the construction sector are also fueling the market growth. In addition, the growing popularity of permeable paving solutions in residential and commercial construction projects is further propelling the market expansion.</p><p>The latest trends in the gravel paver market include the development of advanced permeable paver systems with improved durability and aesthetics. <p><p>The gravel paver market is divided into two main types: tracked pavers and wheeled pavers. Tracked pavers are designed with caterpillar tracks that provide better traction and maneuverability, making them suitable for uneven terrains or off-road conditions. They offer stability and durability but can be slower in terms of speed compared to wheeled pavers. Wheeled pavers, on the other hand, have rubber tires and are faster than tracked pavers. They are typically more suitable for flat or paved surfaces and provide ease of movement and flexibility.</p></p>
