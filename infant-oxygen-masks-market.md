<p><p>Infant oxygen masks are categorized based on whether they have an operation window or not. An operation window refers to a transparent section on the mask that allows healthcare professionals to easily observe the baby's face and monitor their condition. Masks with an operation window provide better visibility and enable medical staff to make adjustments as needed. On the other hand, masks without an operation window do not have a transparent section, making it challenging to observe the infant's face during oxygen administration.</p></p>
