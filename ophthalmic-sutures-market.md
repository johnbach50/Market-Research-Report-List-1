<p><p>Ophthalmic sutures refer to the types of sutures used in ophthalmic surgery, specifically for eye-related procedures. These sutures can be categorized into two types: natural sutures and synthetic sutures. Natural sutures are made from materials like silk or catgut, while synthetic sutures are made from materials like nylon or polypropylene. Both types have their own advantages and disadvantages. Natural sutures are biocompatible but can cause tissue reactions, while synthetic sutures are durable but may not be absorbable by the body. The choice of suture material depends on the specific surgical requirements and surgeon's preference.</p></p>
