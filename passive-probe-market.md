<p><p>A passive probe is an electronic testing device used to measure voltage or current in a circuit without affecting the performance of the circuit. It is typically used in conjunction with oscilloscopes or other measuring instruments to analyze and troubleshoot electronic systems. Unlike active probes, passive probes do not require power and are generally less expensive.</p><p>The market for passive probes is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. The increasing demand for electronic devices, such as smartphones, tablets, and laptops, is driving the need for efficient testing equipment, including passive probes. The rising adoption of Internet of Things (IoT) devices and technologies is also contributing to the growth of the passive probe market. With the increasing complexity of electronic systems, there is a growing need for accurate and reliable testing solutions, which is further fueling the market growth.</p><p>Moreover, the growing focus on research and development activities in the semiconductor industry is driving the demand for passive probes. The market is witnessing a shift towards the adoption of advanced technologies, such as ultra-high bandwidth and high-voltage passive probes. These probes offer improved accuracy, higher bandwidth, and better signal fidelity, which are crucial in industries like aerospace, defense, and automotive.</p><p>Additionally, there is a growing trend of miniaturization in electronic devices, which is driving the demand for compact and lightweight passive probes.
