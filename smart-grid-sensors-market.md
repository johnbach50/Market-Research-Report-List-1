<p><p>The smart grid sensors market can be categorized into two types: cellular sensors and Wi-Fi sensors. Cellular sensors rely on cellular networks for communication and data transmission. They are ideal for remote areas where Wi-Fi connectivity is limited. On the other hand, Wi-Fi sensors use wireless local area networks (LANs) for communication within a specific area. They are commonly used in urban environments or areas with strong Wi-Fi coverage. Both types of sensors play a crucial role in enabling real-time data collection and communication necessary for efficient management of the smart grid.</p></p>
