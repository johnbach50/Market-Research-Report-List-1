<p><p>Yoghurt making culture market comprises different types, including Mesophilic, Thermophilic, and Probiotics. Mesophilic cultures operate at lower temperatures and are commonly used for making soft yoghurts and cheese. On the other hand, Thermophilic cultures thrive at higher temperatures and are ideal for producing Greek or strained yoghurt. Lastly, Probiotics refer to the market segment focused on cultures that provide health benefits, such as improving digestion and boosting the immune system. These three types cater to different yoghurt making processes and offer various attributes and advantages to meet consumer preferences.</p></p>
