<p><p>Diagnostic medical electrodes are used to record bioelectrical signals from the body for diagnostic purposes. Wet electrodes, the most common type, use a conductive gel to improve electrical conduction, ensuring accurate readings. Dry electrodes, a newer technology, eliminate the need for gel by using a conductive material on the electrode surface. Needle electrodes are designed for invasive measurements, inserted directly into muscles or tissues. The different types of electrodes cater to diverse clinical requirements, with wet electrodes being widely used, dry electrodes offering convenience, and needle electrodes used for precise invasive readings.</p></p>
