<p><ul><li>PIR (Passive Infrared) Sensors</li><li>Ultrasonic Sensors</li><li>Dual-technology Occupancy Sensors</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Lighting control occupancy sensors are devices used to detect the presence or absence of people in a space and accordingly control the lighting system. The market for these sensors can be categorized into different types. PIR (Passive Infrared) sensors use thermal detection to sense changes in infrared energy, while ultrasonic sensors emit sound waves and detect their reflection to determine occupancy. Dual-technology occupancy sensors combine both PIR and ultrasonic technologies for increased accuracy. Other types may include microwave sensors, camera-based sensors, or infrared imaging sensors, each having its own unique method of occupancy detection.</p></p>
