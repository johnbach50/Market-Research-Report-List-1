<p><p>A smart card, also known as an IC card or integrated circuit card, is a pocket-sized card embedded with integrated circuits. Smart cards are primarily used in a wide range of applications such as banking, telecom, healthcare, retail, transportation, and government IDs.</p><p>The smart card market has been experiencing steady growth in recent years and is expected to continue to grow at a CAGR of 3.90% during the forecast period. Several factors are driving this growth. One of the main drivers is the increasing adoption of smart cards in the banking sector for secure payment transactions. With the rise in digital payments and the need for secure transactions, smart cards offer a reliable solution that can protect sensitive information and prevent fraud.</p><p>Moreover, the growing demand for secure identification systems in various industries, such as healthcare and government, is also contributing to market growth. Smart cards are widely used for personal identification, access control, and healthcare records management, among other applications. The convenience and security offered by smart cards make them an ideal choice for these industries.</p><p>In addition, the increasing popularity of contactless smart cards is another trend driving market growth. Contactless smart cards use radio frequency technology to enable quick and convenient transactions without the need for physical contact with a reader. This technology is being widely adopted in transportation systems, access control, and e-government applications.</p><p>Overall, the smart card market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years due to the increasing demand for secure and convenient identification and payment solutions. The advancements in technology, such as contactless smart cards, are further propelling this growth.</p></p>
