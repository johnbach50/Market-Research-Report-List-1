<p><p>The low voltage harmonic filter market is segmented into two types: active and passive. </p><p>Active filters use power electronics to detect and mitigate harmonics in real-time. They require energy to operate and are more complex and expensive. </p><p>Passive filters, on the other hand, use passive components like resistors, capacitors, and inductors to filter out harmonic currents. They are relatively simpler in design, cost-effective, and do not consume additional energy. </p><p>Both filter types are used to eliminate or reduce harmonics in electrical systems, improving power quality and preventing damage to equipment.</p></p>
