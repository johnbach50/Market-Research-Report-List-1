<p><p>The Allergy Diagnostic and Treatment Market encompasses two main types of tests: In Vivo Tests and In Vitro Tests. In Vivo Tests involve exposing the patient's skin to potential allergens and observing the allergic reaction. This includes the Skin Prick Test and Patch Test. In Vitro Tests, on the other hand, analyze blood samples to detect specific allergen-specific antibodies, such as the Radioallergosorbent Test (RAST) and Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay (ELISA). Both types of tests play a significant role in diagnosing and treating allergies.</p></p>
