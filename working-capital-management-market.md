<p><p>Working Capital Management refers to the strategies and techniques used by companies to efficiently manage their current assets and liabilities. There are two main market types in this context: Gross Working Capital (GWC) and Net Working Capital (NWC). GWC refers to the total value of a company's current assets, including cash, inventory, and accounts receivable. NWC, on the other hand, deducts the total current liabilities, such as accounts payable and short-term debt, from the GWC, indicating the company's ability to meet its short-term obligations.</p></p>
