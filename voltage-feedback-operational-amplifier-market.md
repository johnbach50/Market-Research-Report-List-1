<p><p>Voltage feedback operational amplifiers (Op-Amps) are electronic devices widely used in various applications. They can be categorized into types based on the type of feedback they utilize: positive feedback and negative feedback.</p><p>Positive feedback amplifiers boost the input signal, increasing the output voltage beyond the input voltage. They are often used to amplify weak signals and provide greater gain. However, they can be prone to instability and oscillations.</p><p>In contrast, negative feedback amplifiers reduce the output signal, resulting in a more controlled and stable amplification. They are widely used in audio amplifiers and other applications where accuracy and stability are crucial, as they minimize distortion and noise.</p></p>
