<p><p>Hydronic underfloor heating systems consist of two main types: dry water floor heating and wet water floor heating. Dry water floor heating involves installing a network of pipes beneath the floor, where warm water circulates to provide even and efficient heat. Wet water floor heating, on the other hand, uses a concrete slab and embedded pipes to distribute heated water throughout the floor. Both systems offer comfortable and energy-efficient heating solutions, but they vary in terms of installation process and cost.</p></p>
