<p><p>Mechanical Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (mCPR) devices are used to provide chest compressions to individuals in cardiac arrest when manual CPR may not be feasible. There are two main types of mCPR devices: electric and pneumatic. Electric mCPR devices are powered by electricity and provide automated chest compressions to maintain blood flow. Pneumatic mCPR devices, on the other hand, use compressed air or gas to deliver chest compressions. Both types aim to sustain circulation during CPR and offer alternatives to manual chest compressions in emergency situations.</p></p>
