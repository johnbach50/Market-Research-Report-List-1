<p><p>HIV-1 testing is a diagnostic procedure that detects the presence of the human immunodeficiency virus (HIV-1) in individuals. Various testing methods are available, including nucleic acid amplification test (NAAT), enzyme immunoassay (EIA), rapid HIV test, and point-of-care testing (POCT). These tests detect HIV-1 antibodies, antigens, or the virus itself in blood, saliva, or urine samples.</p><p>The HIV-1 testing market has witnessed significant growth in recent years due to various factors. <p><ul><li>Antibody</li><li>Nucleic Acid Testing</li><li>Antigen</li></ul></p>
<p><p>HIV-1 testing offers different types of methods to detect the presence of the virus. Antibody testing checks for HIV-1 antibodies produced by the immune system in response to the infection. Nucleic acid testing (NAT) detects the genetic material (RNA) of the virus directly in the blood. Antigen testing identifies HIV-1 antigens, which are proteins produced by the virus. Each type has its advantages and limitations in terms of accuracy, turnaround time, and cost. These testing methods play a crucial role in diagnosing HIV-1 infections and monitoring the effectiveness of treatment.</p></p>
