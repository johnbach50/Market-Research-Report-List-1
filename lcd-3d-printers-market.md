<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>LCD 3D printers are differentiated into two market types: Top Down and Bottom Up. In Top Down printers, the light source is situated at the top and projects the UV light downwards, solidifying the resin layer by layer. This method offers faster print speeds but may cause potential issues with surface quality. Conversely, Bottom Up printers have the light source located beneath the resin tank, solidifying the resin from the bottom upwards. While this approach provides better surface quality, it generally results in slower print speeds compared to Top Down printers.</p></p>
