<p><p>Epilepsy monitoring devices can be categorized into conventional and wearable devices. Conventional devices are typically used in clinical settings and consist of wired electrodes that are attached to the scalp to record brain activities. They require the patient to stay in the hospital or clinic for monitoring. On the other hand, wearable devices are portable and can be worn by the patient at home or on the go. They are more convenient and allow long-term monitoring, providing continuous data for better diagnosis and treatment of epilepsy.</p></p>
