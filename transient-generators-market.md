<p><p>Transient generators, also known as impulse generators, are equipment used to generate short-duration electrical pulses or transients for testing purposes. There are two main types of transient generators: surge generators and burst generators. Surge generators produce high-voltage transients with steep rising slopes, simulating power system disturbances like lightning strikes. On the other hand, burst generators generate transients with fast repetition rates, simulating electromagnetic interference (EMI) events. Both generators play a crucial role in testing the resilience and protection capabilities of electrical systems against transients in various industries, including power generation, telecommunications, and automotive.</p></p>
