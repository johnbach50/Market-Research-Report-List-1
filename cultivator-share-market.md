<p><p>Cultivator share market types include chisel, triangular, and reversible markets. In a chisel market, the cultivator’s share is distributed among the landowner and the cultivator in a ratio negotiated beforehand. A triangular market is similar, but the landowner’s share is determined by a formula based on the total production. Finally, in a reversible market, the cultivator’s share of the produce can either be fixed or determined by a negotiation. These market types outline different ways in which the distribution of the cultivator’s share is managed between the cultivator and the landowner.</p></p>
