<p><p>Lung cancer surgery is a medical procedure performed to remove cancerous tumors or nodules in the lung. It is often considered as the primary treatment option for early-stage lung cancer when the cancer has not spread beyond the lung.</p><p>The procedure can be performed through different methods, including open surgery (thoracotomy) or minimally invasive techniques like video-assisted thoracoscopic surgery (VATS) or robotic-assisted surgery. Lung cancer surgeries include lobectomy (removal of a lobe of the lung), pneumonectomy (removal of an entire lung), segmentectomy (removal of a small part of the lung), or wedge resection (removal of a small, wedge-shaped section of the lung). The choice of surgery depends on various factors, such as the size and location of the tumor and the overall health of the patient.</p><p>The lung cancer surgery market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. The increasing incidence of lung cancer, advancements in surgical techniques and technologies, and rising awareness about early diagnosis are major factors contributing to the market growth. Additionally, the growing geriatric population, who are more prone to lung cancer, is also boosting the demand for lung cancer surgery procedures.</p><p>Moreover, the market is experiencing several trends that are shaping its growth. The introduction of minimally invasive surgical techniques has revolutionized the field of lung cancer surgery, as it offers reduced post-operative pain, shorter hospital stays, and faster recovery.
