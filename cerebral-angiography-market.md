<p><ul><li>Magnetic Resonance Imaging</li><li>Computed Tomography</li><li>Ultrasound</li><li>Nuclear Imaging</li><li>X-ray</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Cerebral angiography refers to a medical procedure that allows the visualization of blood vessels within the brain. Various imaging techniques are used for this purpose. Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) utilizes powerful magnets and radio waves to create detailed images of blood vessels. Computed Tomography (CT) employs X-ray technology to generate cross-sectional images. Ultrasound uses sound waves to produce images of blood vessels. Nuclear imaging involves injecting a small amount of radioactive material into the bloodstream and using a special camera to capture images. X-ray angiography employs contrast dye and X-rays to create images of blood vessels. These techniques help in diagnosing and treating cerebrovascular diseases.</p></p>
