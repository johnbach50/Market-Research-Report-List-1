<p><p>Printed electronics is a fast-growing market that encompasses various printing technologies. Inkjet printing involves spraying ink onto a substrate, allowing for precise and customizable designs. Gravure printing utilizes engraved cylinders to transfer ink onto the material, ideal for large-scale production. Screen printing uses a mesh stencil to apply ink, suitable for thicker surfaces like ceramics. Flexography employs flexible printing plates, delivering high-speed and efficient printing on materials like plastic films. Other types include offset lithography, which offers high-quality printing, and 3D printing, which enables the production of three-dimensional electronic components.</p></p>
