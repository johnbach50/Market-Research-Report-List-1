<p><p>Female urinary incontinence refers to the involuntary leakage of urine, which can significantly impact the quality of life of affected individuals. There are several treatment options available for this condition, ranging from non-invasive interventions to surgical procedures.</p><p>Non-invasive treatments include pelvic muscle exercises (Kegel exercises), behavior modification techniques, and the use of devices such as pessaries. Medications such as anticholinergics and mirabegron can also be prescribed to manage symptoms. For more severe cases, surgical options like sling procedures, bladder neck suspension, or artificial urinary sphincter placement may be recommended.</p><p>The global female urinary incontinence treatment market is projected to witness significant growth in the coming years.
