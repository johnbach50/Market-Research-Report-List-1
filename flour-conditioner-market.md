<p><p>The flour conditioner market consists of two main types: powder and liquid. Powder flour conditioners are dry mixtures that are easily dissolved in water to create a conditioning solution. They are often packaged in small sachets or tubs. Liquid flour conditioners, on the other hand, come in liquid form and can be directly added to flour or dough during the baking process. They are typically sold in bottles or cans. Both powder and liquid flour conditioners serve the purpose of improving the quality and texture of baked goods by enhancing dough elasticity and strength.</p></p>
