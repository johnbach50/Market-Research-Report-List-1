<p><p>Small molecule targeted cancer therapy refers to the use of drugs that specifically target molecules involved in the growth and spread of cancer cells. The market for such therapies can be classified into three main types based on the type of drugs used. Monoclonal antibodies are proteins that target and bind to specific antigens on cancer cells to inhibit their growth. Small molecules are low molecular weight compounds that can penetrate cells and interfere with specific cancer-related pathways. Small molecule drug conjugates are small molecules linked to a drug payload that selectively target cancer cells.</p></p>
